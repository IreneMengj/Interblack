Magnesia Flux

Description

Light-burned magnesia balls are created by pressing mixed light-burned magnesia powder into balls. They are used primarily in converter steelmaking to splash slag, protecting the furnace and extending its life.

  • Preparation: 80% magnesia powder mixed with 20% water, pressed for 15-20 minutes. The balls are then formed and air-dried for 48 hours to reach saleable strength.
  • Function: Acts as a slag component regulator, replacing dolomite to adjust slag viscosity and refractoriness, boosting MgO content while reducing slag quantity.
  • Benefits: Higher MgO content raises melting point (2800°C), increasing slag viscosity and refractoriness. This enables the slag to bond with furnace lining, forming a protective layer that extends furnace life.
  • Industrial Success: U.S. steel companies using this technique achieve up to 20,000 heats and 97% converter operation rate. Widely adopted in countries like Germany, South Korea, Japan, and Taiwan, with companies such as Taiyuan Steel and Chengde Steel reporting extended furnace lives up to 12,000 heats and significant economic gains.

Requirements for Magnesia Flux

Parameter Requirement
MgO content ≥ 80%
CaO content ≤ 8%
SiO2 content ≤ 2.5%
Fe2O3 content ≤ 8%
Fractional composition ≥ 90% of the batch 10-50 mm
State Ready for use

Converter Slag Characteristics

Characteristic Value
Slag basicity (CaO + MgO) / SiO2 ≥ 2.3
Total FeO content in slag ≤ 18%
Total MgO content in slag ≥ 4%
Volume of converter slag per melt m³ 6-7
